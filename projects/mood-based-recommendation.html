Product Case Study

Mood-Based Food Recommendation

Food delivery platforms are built around search and categories. But most users don’t open an app thinking “I want North Indian.” They open it thinking “I feel like something comforting” or “I want something light.” The gap between emotion and structured menus creates friction in discovery.

The Problem

Users experience decision fatigue when scrolling through large restaurant catalogs. Static recommendations based on popularity or past orders ignore real-time context. This reduces engagement depth and slows conversion.

The Insight

Mood is a contextual signal. It changes dynamically and influences cuisine preference, price tolerance, and exploration behavior. Instead of optimizing only for historical frequency, recommendations should adapt to emotional intent.

The Solution

I designed a mood-driven recommendation layer that maps emotional input into weighted cuisine clusters. The engine blends session timing, behavioral history, and preference decay to surface contextually relevant dishes.

Rather than showing “Top Rated,” the interface guides users through mood journeys — Comfort, Celebration, Light & Healthy, Indulgent, etc. This restructures discovery from search-heavy to intent-led navigation.

Measurement Framework

Success is evaluated through measurable product metrics: click-through rate uplift, reduction in scroll depth before selection, increase in repeat orders, and session-to-order conversion rate. Experiment design includes A/B testing against standard recommendation logic.

Why This Matters

This feature transforms recommendations from passive ranking systems into dynamic engagement levers. By aligning emotional context with measurable KPIs, the product shifts from transactional ordering to experiential discovery — improving both retention and long-term value.
